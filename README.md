# Excel-Finance-Analytics

This repository contains a set of financial reports I created in **Microsoft Excel** as part of my learning project using AtliQ Hardwares sales data to analyze sales, profitability, and gross margin performance.

These reports demonstrate my ability to use Excel for **data analysis, business reporting, performance tracking, and dashboard-style presentation**.

---

## Reports Included
### 1. P & L Report
A profit and loss summary by fiscal year, showing:
- Net Sales
- COGS
- Gross Margin
- Gross Margin %
- Year-over-year growth

**Key Highlights:** While Net Sales tripled from 2020 to 2021, Gross Margin % declined slightly from 37.3% to 36.4%, indicating that costs grew marginally faster than revenue — a key insight for business decision-making.

### 2. P & L Monthly Report
A detailed monthly breakdown of profit and loss metrics by fiscal month (**Sept - Ãug**) for each fiscal year, including:
- Monthly Net Sales
- Monthly COGS
- Monthly Gross Margin
- Gross Margin %
- Comparison across fiscal years

**Key Highlights:** 
- Q2 (Nov–Dec) consistently delivers the highest revenue — peak season effect
- In FY 2020, March saw an unusual dip to $2.1M in Net Sales, likely reflecting COVID-19 disruption
- FY 2021 Net Sales in March rebounded dramatically to $44.0M — a 1981.6% jump vs prior year
- Gross Margin % remained remarkably stable month-to-month within each year, staying within a ~1% band

### 3. P & L for Markets
A market-level profit and loss report for FY 2021 that compares financial performance across different countries/markets.

It allows users to evaluate:
- Net Sales by market
- COGS by market
- Gross Margin by market
- Gross Margin % by market

**Key Highlights:** New Zealand and Japan deliver the best profitability ratios despite lower overall revenue. Germany and Norway are high-volume markets but with significantly compressed margins — signalling potential cost inefficiencies or pricing pressure.

### 4. GM% for Quarter
A quarterly gross margin percentage report across 6 geographic subzones (ANZ, India, NA, NE, ROA, SE) for each fiscal year from **2019** to **2021**.

This report helps track:
- Quarterly GM% performance
- Sub-zone comparison
- Gross margin trends across fiscal years

**Key Highlights:** India saw the sharpest GM% decline between 2019 and 2020, dropping from 42.4% to 32.2% — likely driven by aggressive pricing or increased distribution costs. Northern Europe (NE) is the only sub-zone showing consistent improvement across all three years.
